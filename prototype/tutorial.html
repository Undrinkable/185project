RNA

RNA is a linear molecule that consists of four different building blocks: A, U, C, and G.

Binding

RNA can fold back on itself by forming hydrogen bonds between the building blocks in the backbone. A can only bond to U, and vice versa. C can only bond to G and vice versa.

Hairpin loop

One of the most important secondary structures RNA can form are hairpin loops.

Interior loop

Interior loops occur when some bases in a long stretch of pairings don't match.

Bulge loop

Bulges happen when some bases don't have a match in the continuous complementary part of the strand, with everything else matching.

Multi-branch loop

RNA can form complex structures by multi-branching. This is a tRNA.
